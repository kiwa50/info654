Paper making DIY

Making paper was a fun, but laborious activity. I wrote ALOT of letters to friends during the quarantine. My friend, Nina has a typewriter and I thought it would be a fun idea to send her handmade paper that she could use her typewriter to type out a letter, and send back to me. I treasure these collaborative artworks.

I followed the steps in this helpful tutorial on how to make a mould and deckle which is handy in the creation of your handmade paper. A mould and deckle will be a sort of portrait frame for your paper. You'll need chicken wire, a staple gun, duct tape and an old picture frame.


Some more paper projects

To achieve an 'organic' look, I started playing around with the drying process. I would dry the paper sheets on a wool sweater or a textured surface. Typically, cutting up the paper, making the slurry, pouring it into the mould and deckle, and then adding pressed flowers would take four hours. The drying process would takea full night, and even after it's been fully dried, paper warps, so I would then press the paper sheets in between heavy books, and leave them there for an additional day to flatten out.
